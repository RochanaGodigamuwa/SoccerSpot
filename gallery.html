Greatest football legends in the history of the game

Pelé

The only athlete in history to win three FIFA World Cups is Pele. He burst onto the scene in the 1958 World Cup as a 17-year old and was crucial in his country's first World Cup victory. Pele was named the best performer at the 1970 World Cup, and two more titles came along in 1962 and 1970. Not to mention that the 1970 World Cup champions are regarded as the top international football squad.

Diego Maradona

The last time Argentina won the World Cup was in 1986,Maradona was the captain who guided his team to victory. Maradona was an example of a rebel, famous for his explosive temper but also revered by many for his extraordinary ability. He had the ability to outdribble opponents like a hot scalpel through butter. He could single out a teammate with an incredible pass that would confound defenses, and he could consistently create magical moments.

Lionel Messi

Numerous Argentine athletes have claimed to be "The Next Maradona." Ariel Ortega, Pablo Aimar, and many others are examples. None of them lived up to that title. It's arguable that Lionel Messi has eclipsed both the incumbent and the person. One of the most well-known athletes in the world, Messi is a recognized star on a worldwide scale. The rivalry between he and Cristiano Ronaldo has been compared to well-known sporting rivals. He is undoubtedly one of the best sports legends.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Ronaldo became famous around the world because of his outstanding ability and, of course his records, have altered the football landscape. The Portuguese superstar began his professional career at Sporting Lisbon but rose to international prominence with Manchester United, where he went on to capture three Premier League championships and an FA Cup. With them, he won the 2007–2008 UEFA Champions League for the first time. He also won the first of his five Ballon D'Ors in 2008.

Johan Cruyff

Johan Cruyff is on this list even though the other players are known for their records because of the sheer impact he has had on the contemporary game. The Netherlands' ascent to football aristocracy was sparked by Cruyff, an Amsterdam native who was the sport's shining star. Cruyff was the deep-lying forward Rinus Michels used to create his totaalvoetball game, and he was responsible for the formation and plan.

Zinedine Zidane

Without a question, one of the greatest attacking midfielders in history has been Zinedine Zidane. After beginning his football career with the French team Cannes, Zidane transferred to Bordeaux in 1992. The Frenchman earned Ligue 1 Player of the Year in his final season with the Girondins. Following a strong showing for Bordeaux, Zidane received offers from major European teams.

Paolo Maldini

Paolo Maldini only owned one team. The 24-year tenure of the Italian defender in Serie A was spent with AC Milan. Maldini earned a total of 25 trophies while playing for Milan, including five UEFA Champions Leagues, seven Serie A championships, one Coppa Italia, five Supercoppa Italiana crowns, four European Super Cups, two Intercontinental Cups, and one FIFA Club World Cup. Due to his guidance and defensive prowess on the field, the Italian defender was well-liked by all.

Ronaldo Nazario

Even though some might contest his superiority to Pele, you simply cannot help but be amazed when you watch him perform. If not for injuries, The "Fenomeno" would have surpassed all goal-scoring marks, despite his talent and ability. He was regarded as one of history's most versatile attackers. He was able to score goals and help his colleagues by using his incredible dribbling and skills.

Robert Lewandowski

The ex-Borussia Dortmund player is the model for all modern attackers. He is a complete attacker, with a picture-perfect finishing touch, strong shots, dribbles, and amazing set-piece skills. The second-highest goal scorer for Bayern Munich and Poland overall is Robert Lewandowski.

Gianluigi Buffon

One of the all-time greatest goalkeepers in this competition would be Gianluigi Buffon. The legendary Italian spent six seasons at Parma, where he began his professional journey in 1995. Buffon earned one Coppa Italia and one UEFA Euro Cup during his six-year stay with the Crusaders. Buffon flourished and found the majority of his professional success with Juventus.

Thierry Henry

The greatest athlete to ever compete in the Premier League is still Thierry Henry. The Arsenal striker was unstoppable in his heyday and a defensive nightmare because of his lightning-quick speed and agility. The Frenchman, who is regarded as one of the all-time greatest goal scorers, scored more goals for his country than any other player in the annals of French football.

Romario

Romario enjoyed a distinguished career. He has scored the second-most goals in history. He joined the Johan Cruyff dream squad because of his dribbling, ability to maneuver in tight spaces in the penalty area, and finishing. Despite the numerous controversies that dogged his tenure, it was clear that the fans enjoyed his game.
